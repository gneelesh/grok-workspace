## Gaps & Risks

### 1. Resume positioning is builder-first, not seller-first

Current bullets emphasize **what was built** (CI/CD, APIs, ETL) rather than:

- Who the customer was (industry, size — even anonymized)
- What problem was solved (business outcome)
- What alternatives were evaluated (architecture trade-offs)
- What the POC proved (success criteria, time-to-value)

Hiring managers for SE roles scan for **discovery, demos, proposals, technical wins** — not sprint velocity alone.

### 2. No formal sales-cycle exposure

Missing signals that pure sales and senior SA roles expect:

- RFP / RFQ response support
- Architecture workshops with customer architects
- Sizing, BOM, or cost/TCO conversations
- Partner ecosystem / marketplace motions
- Quota or pipeline contribution (even indirect)

This is **expected at 2 YOE** — not a disqualifier for SE, but a reason to avoid jumping straight to AE or Principal SA.

### 3. Thin enterprise architecture narrative

Technically capable, but resume lacks:

- Landing zone / multi-account governance language
- Security compliance frameworks (FedRAMP, HIPAA, SOC2) — even if adjacent
- Large-scale migration or modernization stories
- Stakeholder management above engineering level

Associate SA at partners is still reachable; **principal SA at OCI/IBM** typically requires 4–7+ years.

### 4. Project section under-leverages sales value

Projects are strong technically (F1 scores, LoRA, uptime) but read as **academic/portfolio** work. For pre-sales, they should be framed as **demo assets** and **proof of depth** in AI and cloud delivery.

### 5. Minor resume quality issues

- Typo: `MCPl` → likely **MCP** (Model Context Protocol) or **MLOps**
- Bullet formatting in PDF is inconsistent (empty bullets with text below)
- No professional summary or target title
- No LinkedIn/GitHub URLs (placeholders only)
- Skills list is long but not prioritized for buyer conversations
